# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all community members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

We encourage:

✅ **Be Respectful** - Treat everyone with courtesy and dignity  
✅ **Be Inclusive** - Welcome diverse perspectives and backgrounds  
✅ **Be Constructive** - Provide helpful feedback and criticism  
✅ **Be Collaborative** - Work together toward common goals  
✅ **Be Professional** - Maintain a high standard of conduct  
✅ **Be Patient** - Understand that people are learning  

### Unacceptable Behavior

❌ **Harassment** - Unwanted comments, threats, or intimidation related to protected characteristics  
❌ **Discrimination** - Treating others unfairly based on protected characteristics  
❌ **Abuse** - Insulting, demeaning, or hostile language or conduct  
❌ **Spam** - Repeated unwanted messages or self-promotion  
❌ **Doxxing** - Publishing private information without consent  
❌ **Trolling** - Intentionally posting inflammatory or off-topic content  
❌ **Sexual Harassment** - Unwanted sexual advances, requests, or comments  
❌ **Retaliation** - Punishing someone for reporting violations  

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All community spaces: GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions
- Project communications: email, chat, forums
- Events: workshops, conferences, meetups
- Online and offline interactions related to the project

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you witness or experience a violation, please report it:

📧 **Email:** [ernestzdunczyk@gmail.com](mailto:ernestzdunczyk@gmail.com)  
**Subject:** `[CODE OF CONDUCT] Report - [Brief Description]`

**Include:**
- Description of the incident
- When and where it occurred
- People involved (if safe to share)
- Impact on you or others
- Any evidence (screenshots, messages)
- How you'd like it to be resolved

**All reports are confidential.**

### Investigation Process

1. **Acknowledgment** - We'll confirm receipt within 24 hours
2. **Investigation** - We'll gather information and talk to those involved
3. **Decision** - We'll determine appropriate action
4. **Resolution** - We'll implement the remedy
5. **Notification** - We'll inform all parties of the outcome

### Consequences

Depending on severity and history, consequences may include:

- **Warning** - Private communication about the violation
- **Temporary Ban** - Suspension from community for a set period (7-30 days)
- **Permanent Ban** - Removal from community for serious or repeated violations
- **Legal Action** - Reporting to authorities if illegal activity is involved

---

## Appeals

If you disagree with an enforcement decision, you may appeal:

📧 **Email:** [ernestzdunczyk@gmail.com](mailto:ernestzdunczyk@gmail.com) with subject `[APPEAL] Code of Conduct Decision`

**Appeal should include:**
- Original violation
- Enforcement action taken
- Why you believe it was unjust
- Any new information

## Guidelines for Contributors

### In Issues and Discussions

✅ Use respectful language  
✅ Assume good intent  
✅ Ask clarifying questions before criticizing  
✅ Offer constructive suggestions  
✅ Stay on topic  

❌ Don't dismiss ideas without consideration  
❌ Don't use offensive language or slurs  
❌ Don't gatekeep or belittle  
❌ Don't share others' private information  

### In Code Reviews

✅ Focus on the code, not the person  
✅ Provide specific, actionable feedback  
✅ Praise good work  
✅ Acknowledge effort and learning  

❌ Don't make personal attacks  
❌ Don't nitpick trivial issues  
❌ Don't demand changes imperiously  
❌ Don't block PRs for stylistic preferences  

### In Documentation

✅ Use inclusive language  
✅ Provide examples for different learning styles  
✅ Consider accessibility  

❌ Don't use gendered pronouns without reason  
❌ Don't use jargon without explanation  
❌ Don't assume user demographics

## Inclusive Language

### Examples

| Instead of | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| "guys" | "everyone", "folks", "team" |
| "man-hours" | "person-hours", "time" |
| "blacklist/whitelist" | "blocklist/allowlist" |
| "master/slave" | "primary/replica", "main/secondary" |
| "crazy", "insane" | "surprising", "unexpected", "complex" |
| "disabled" (for code) | "inactive", "unavailable" |
